The Frankfurt Auschwitz Trial was a landmark event held in West Germany, marking the prosecution of 22 former SS personnel responsible for atrocities at Auschwitz. This trial was pivotal in confronting Germany's Nazi past, providing detailed testimony that significantly influenced public awareness and understanding of the Holocaust.

Horst Fischer Trial (1965, East Germany)

The Horst Fischer Trial was held in East Germany, prosecuting Horst Fischer, an SS doctor accused of war crimes at Auschwitz. Fischer was sentenced to death and executed in 1967. This trial served as a political statement by the GDR, emphasizing its antifascist stance and distinguishing its judicial approach from West Germany.
